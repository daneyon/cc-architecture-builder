Block 1 — The Shift 05

Core Concept

What is context engineering?

Context Engineering

Curating what the model sees so you get a better result. Not just the prompt—the complete information architecture: system instructions, tool definitions, retrieved knowledge, conversation history, and output constraints.

  • Prompt engineering: "How do I phrase this better?"
  • Context engineering: "What does the AI need to decide correctly, and how do I structure it?"
  • One helps you once. The other helps you every time.
  • You already do this when you scope a project or write a technical memo
  • A new way of thinking about work—not a new tool to learn
  • Being the context engineer is exercising high agency
Boeckeler/Fowler, Thoughtworks, 2026  |  Koylan, "The File System Is the New Database," 2025

Key Design Principles

01 Separation of concerns — personal config vs. project knowledge
02 Progressive disclosure — load only what’s needed, when triggered
03 Convention over configuration — sane defaults, override when needed

Block 2 — The Practice 11

Practical Framework

Mapping workflows to AI collaboration

Identify highest-bottleneck tasks

  • Repetitive — same steps, different inputs
  • Time-intensive — high effort, moderate complexity
  • Error-prone — human fatigue matters

Traditional gap analysis methods (process mapping, SIPOC, value stream) still apply—AI skill is reframing them into an architecture.

The 20-60-20 working heuristic

20% Human
60% AI-Assisted
20% Human
  • 20% — Context design, scoping, intent framing
  • 60% — AI-assisted drafting and execution
  • 20% — Human review, domain validation, QA

Not a fixed rule—an adaptive starting point. Ratios shift with task complexity and AI maturity.

System Design Vision

Human-AI architecture

Human Orchestrator Layer
Strategic oversight · Context design · Quality judgment · Domain expertise · Ethical review
↓ Designs context, sets guardrails, validates outputs ↓
Human-in-the-Loop
Data Management Agent
Format conversion, QA checks, data cleaning. Why: Outputs are objectively verifiable; human approves each batch before downstream use.
Human-on-the-Loop
Analysis Agent
Report drafting, compliance scanning, literature synthesis. Why: Requires domain judgment; human reviews outputs, intervenes on exceptions.
Human-Directed
Monitoring Agent
Real-time data feeds, alerting, notifications. Why: Operates within human-set thresholds; human defines rules, not each action.
↓ Progressive autonomy as trust, validation, and guardrail maturity increase ↓

Terminology follows EU AI Act / NIST conventions: “in-the-loop” (active co-decision), “on-the-loop” (monitor + intervene), “directed” (set policy, not each action).

Block 3 — Guardrails 20

Closing the Loop

Human orchestration is an
engineering constraint

  • Masters et al. (2025): GPT-5 Manager Agents couldn’t jointly optimize across 20 workflows. A measured engineering limitation, not philosophy.
  • The closed loop: Orchestrator (Block 1) structures knowledge (Block 2); the guardrail IS the quality of that orchestration (Block 3).

Knowledge Democratization

Base AI capability is free or near-free. The question for private consultancies: if everyone can access the same models, what’s your moat?

The answer is proprietary context engineering—structured domain knowledge, validated workflows, and quality standards that take years to accumulate and cannot be replicated by a prompt.

Block 3 — The Horizon 21

Business Strategy

The Second Act

Every services business faces a transition: from project delivery (sell hours, deliver scope) to product-enabled delivery (sell outcomes, scale expertise through systems).

  • Act 1: Deliver projects. Expertise lives in people. Revenue scales linearly with headcount.
  • The Transition: Project management → product management. Systematize knowledge, standardize workflows, build reusable AI-enhanced tools.
  • Act 2: Deliver products and product-enabled services. Expertise lives in systems. Revenue potential scales non-linearly.
Jevons Paradox: AI efficiency gains create more demand for human expertise, not less. The pie grows.
Five Strategies to Catalyze the Transition
