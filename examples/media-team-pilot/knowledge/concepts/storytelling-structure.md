# Storytelling Structure for Short-Form Video

## Three-Act Structure (Adapted for 1-3 Minute Videos)

### Act 1: Hook & Setup (15-20% of duration)
- **Scene 1-2**: Grab attention immediately with a compelling image or question
- Establish the world, mood, and central question
- The viewer should know what this video is *about* within 10 seconds

### Act 2: Development (60-70% of duration)
- **Scenes 3-7**: Build the narrative with escalating interest
- Each scene should advance the story or deepen understanding
- Vary the visual pace — alternate between wide and close compositions
- Use narration to guide, visuals to *show*

### Act 3: Resolution (15-20% of duration)
- **Scene 8-10**: Deliver the payoff or conclusion
- The final image should be memorable — it's what lingers
- Narration should close with resonance, not summary

## Visual Storytelling Principles

### Show, Don't Tell
- Narration and visuals should complement, not duplicate
- If the narration says "a vast ocean," the image should *evoke* vastness, not just show water

### Visual Consistency
- All scenes should feel like they exist in the same world
- Maintain consistent: color palette, art style, lighting quality, level of detail
- The global style prefix in image prompts enforces this

### Emotional Arc
- Even in short videos, create an emotional journey
- Build tension, wonder, or curiosity before resolving it
- Music mood should track the emotional arc

## Scene Design Guidelines

| Element | Guideline |
|---------|-----------|
| Duration | 5-15 seconds per scene |
| Narration | 15-40 words per scene (~150 words/minute pace) |
| Visual description | 20-50 words, focused on composition |
| Transitions | Crossfade between scenes, fade-in for first, fade-out for last |
| Scene count | 5-12 scenes for 1-3 minute videos |
